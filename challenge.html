Problem Space

There are five thousand shelters in the United States, and two thirds of our population today live with animals.

Research

While I have toyed around with the idea, I have yet to adopt a pet. I decided to invest time in up front research. I wanted to get a hollistic understanding of the process to better identify the problem I wanted to solve.

Primary Research

I conducted 3 interviews with current pet owners and attended an adoption event to do a bit of ethnographic research. In addition, I pulled on studies, newspaper, and shelter materials to better understand the problem space.

Retrospective

I have always had an interest in packaging design and this was a great way to familiarize myself with the field. While at a small scale, I was able to think about the full process. From baking to packaging assembly, this project allowed me to think deeply about materials, costs, and the greater goals of the brand and client. In the future I hope to work on projects that are holistic like this, and that welcome design into all stages of the process.

Sources

Allen, Greg. “With Rescue Dogs In Demand, More Shelters Look Far Afield For Fido.” NPR, NPR, 1 Jan. 2015, www.npr.org/2015/01/01/374257591/with-rescue-dogs-in-demand-more-shelters-look-far-afield-for-fido.

Article covering the regionality of animals in need and the trend to transport them to keep up with demand. Discusses implications of practice, including animal health and logevity.

Amiot, Catherine E, and Brock Bastian. “Toward a Psychology of Human-Animal Relations.”Psychological Bulletin, 3 Nov. 2014, pp. 1–42.

Study looking at zoo donations and “willingness to pay” comparing affect-related (charisma, etc) to biological characteristics (threat level, endangered, phylogenetic distance from humans).

ASPCA. “What Kind of Dog Is Right for You?” Petfinder, www.petfinder.com/pet-adoption/dog-adoption/type-dog-adoption/.

Educational material for pairing personality, lifestyle and maintenance of dog to owner from popular adoption resource.

Brandt, Karon. “Why Are Dogs Given Up?” Petfinder, www.petfinder.com/pet-adoption/dog-adoption/pets-relinquished-shelters/.

Study conducted by National Council on Pet Population Study and Policy, covering top ten reasons for dog and cat relinquishment and their characteristics.

Garrison, Laurie, and Emily Weiss. What Do People Want? Factors People Consider When Acquiring Dogs, the Complexity of the Choices They Make, and Implications for Nonhuman Animal Relocation Programs. Journal of Applied Animal Welfare Science, 16 Aug. 2014.

Abstract covered efficacy of animal “profiles” including attributes of age, size, color, origin, and euthanasia risk. Found that no one driver for adoption, and that diversity in animal over desirability proven to be more appealing to adopter. Recommended shelters diversify.

Healy/Boulder, Rita. “A Personality Test for Pets.” Time, 14 Dec. 2007, content.time.com/time/nation/article/0,8599,1694804,00.html.

Article covered the process of a shelter using an assessment to pair adopters with animals in their possession. Assessment was focused on applicant’s lifestyle, expectations, etc and matched based on suitableness to animal’s personality and needs.

Lampe, Rachel, and Thomas H. Witte. “Speed of Dog Adoption: Impact of Online Photo Traits.” Journal of Applied Animal Welfare Science, vol. 18, no. 4, Dec. 2014, pp. 343–354., doi:10.1080/10888705.2014.982796.

Abstract discussed the effectiveness and impact of dog photographs on adoption sights. Direct correlation to adoption.

Lang, Cady. “'Keeping Up With the Kattarshians' Is the Cat Reality TV Show You Never Knew You Needed.” Time, 21 Feb. 2017, time.com/4677589/keeping-up-with-the-kattarshians/.

Article covers Iceland streaming-phenomenon, inspired by U.S. reality tv show and featuring cats up for adoption. Found to elevate the status of adoption and increase rate of adoption in Iceland.

Levine, Alexandra S. “How to Adopt a Pet.” The New York Times, The New York Times, 11 May 2017, www.nytimes.com/2017/05/11/nyregion/new-york-today-how-to-adopt-a-pet.html?_r=0.

Educational resource covered basic steps or stages of animal adoption process including: map expectations, research, meet animal, adopt. Provided overview into process for potential adopters.

McGarrity, Monica E, et al. “Which Personality Dimensions Do Puppy Tests Measure? A Systematic Procedure for Categorizing Behavioral Assays.” El Sevier, 30 Sept. 2014.

Reviewed and classified 264 behavioral assays to identify categories of dog personality.

Riemer, Stefanie, et al. “The Predictive Value of Early Behavioural Assessments in Pet Dogs – A Longitudinal Study from Neonates to Adults.” PLOS ONE, Public Library of Science, 8 July 2014, journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0101237.

Research study examining the trend to asses puppy personalities. Found efficacy in policing and service dogs, but questioned companion effectiveness. Assessed personality at three stages, 2 to 10 days, 40 to 50 days, and 1.5 to 2 years.

O'Connor, Rachel, et al. “Effect of Adopters' Lifestyles and Animal-Care Knowledge on Their Expectations Prior to Companion-Animal Guardianship.” Journal of Applied Animal Welfare Science, vol. 19, no. 2, Nov. 2016, pp. 157–170., doi:10.1080/10888705.2015.1125295

Study covered 20 shelters in Ontario, Canada. Surveyed adopters lifestyle, expectations, and knowledge of animal care. Found that dog adopter’s expectations on animal behavior and impact on social/relationship statuses to be higher. Highlighted expectation mapping as critical to successful matching and outcomes.
